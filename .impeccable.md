# Yuval — Design Context

> A bilingual (Hebrew / English) digital reading sanctuary built on top of BookForge. This document is the single source of truth for design intent. If a decision isn't represented here, it hasn't been made yet. If the code contradicts this document, the code is wrong.

---

## Design Context

### Users

Readers — primarily Hebrew-first, bilingual-capable — who have chosen to sit with a book. They are not "users completing a task." They are not on the platform to be productive, to be converted, or to be re-engaged. They arrived because they wanted to read, and the interface's only job is to honor that.

Sessions are long (15–90 minutes), often unbroken, often at the edges of the day: morning coffee, lunch pause, the hour before sleep. The product is read in all three lighting contexts in the same week, by the same person. The theme must follow the reader, not ask them.

**Job to be done:** disappear. Everything the interface does, it does in service of the page. The moment the reader becomes aware of the chrome, the chrome has failed.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **carved, quiet, consequential**.

- **Carved** — not rendered. Every surface, every glyph, every edge looks like it was made with intent by a hand that cared. Nothing is procedural. Nothing is templated. Typography in particular must feel like stone-cut or letterpressed type, not a web font at 400 weight.
- **Quiet** — the product never raises its voice. No toasts that bounce, no confetti, no tooltips that beg for attention, no "Did you know?" prompts. Feedback is given through the smallest possible gesture that works.
- **Consequential** — every pixel earns its place. If an element cannot justify itself against "does this make the reader more present in the book?", it is removed. Features are not added because they are expected (theme toggles, share buttons, search bars); they are added because they serve the text.

Emotional goal: the reader closes the tab feeling the way they feel after closing a well-bound book — a little slower, a little fuller. Not "engaged."

### Aesthetic Direction

The product leads its category. It does not reference Kindle, Medium, Substack, Apple Books, Readwise, or any "web app" aesthetic. It references printed artifacts: a museum exhibition catalog, a Steidl photo book, a hand-bound siddur, an Adler & Van Essche edition of Borges, the inside cover of a book that has been rebound by a craftsman.

**The four directives below are non-negotiable. They are the product.**

---

#### 1. Adaptive Atmosphere (theme)

There is no theme toggle. There is no "light / dark / sepia" picker in a settings menu. Theme is a continuous, ambient property of the reading session, not a discrete choice.

**Inputs that move the atmosphere:**
- Local time of day (wall-clock, IANA timezone, not UTC).
- Elapsed reading session length (the longer the session, the deeper the paper tone drifts).
- System `prefers-color-scheme` (used as a ceiling, never as the single signal).
- A "hold" gesture the reader can invoke to freeze the current atmosphere for this session only — never written to a toggle, never persisted as a preference.

**The atmosphere:**
- Surfaces interpolate continuously between two named anchors — **First Light** (a crisp, slightly cool paper near #f7f3ea) and **Midnight** (a deep ink near #0d0d0f with a warm graphite cast). Intermediate states are real computed values, not "three themes crossfading."
- Text color, border color, highlight ink, and link ink all travel on their own curves, not locked to the background. Ink reaches its darkest saturation before the paper reaches its lightest — the way ink behaves on real paper as the light changes.
- Transitions are **slow**: minimum 40 seconds for a perceptible step, minutes for a full traversal. Never cross-fade a theme class swap. The entire color system is expressed as an interpolation against a single `--yuval-atmosphere` scalar token (0 = First Light, 1 = Midnight).
- **Reduced-motion users** get the atmosphere snapped to the value appropriate for the current time-of-day, without drift. They never lose the effect — they lose the animation of it.

Sepia is retained as an accessibility option for users with specific ocular sensitivity, not as a "mood." It is reached through the reader's settings, not through the atmosphere system.

---

#### 2. The Living Margin (differentiation)

Marginalia is the product's signature. It is what the reader will remember. It must be structurally different from every other reading product on the web.

**Two expressions:**

**A. Ink-on-paper bleed.** Highlights, annotations, and bookmarks are not CSS background colors with a border-radius. They are rendered through an SVG `<filter>` composition — `feTurbulence` + `feDisplacementMap` + `feGaussianBlur` at a very low scale (displacement ≤ 1.5px) — so the edge of a highlight is never perfectly rectangular. It bleeds into the grain of the paper by one or two pixels of displacement, asymmetric on the top vs. bottom edge. This must work on both light and dark atmosphere ends (ink bleeds *darker* on light paper, *lighter* on dark ink). Cost budget: filter work is done once per highlight on mount, never re-applied on scroll.

**B. Density Map navigation.** Book progress is not a percentage bar. It is a generative visual: the entire book rendered as a single SVG column where every chapter is a region, every paragraph is a line, and the reader's own activity — read-through, bookmarks, highlights, annotations — deposits visible marks on the map over time. Reading a chapter slowly deepens its ink. Highlights appear as small marks at their true y-position within the chapter. The reader's session is visible as a faint trail. A finished book is an object — a print, a reproducible artifact — that belongs to that reader.

The Density Map replaces the standard table-of-contents sidebar. There is no secondary "fallback" TOC with chapter titles in a list; the map IS the navigation. Chapter titles appear on hover / focus / tap, never by default.

#### 3. Typography — Carved, not Rendered

**Hebrew display face:** a boutique, authoritative Hebrew display type from an independent foundry (candidates: Pre-Sale's *Brnkl*, Fontef's *Narkisim Ultra / Schocken*, HaTypography's *Greta Hebrew*, or a Masterfont display cut). The face must have a visible pen or chisel logic — terminals, modulation, and optical sizing that read as *made*, not *generated*. Fraunces, Newsreader, Frank Ruhl Libre at default weight, and any Google Fonts reflex Hebrew serif are disqualified at the brief.

Frank Ruhl Libre is currently installed and is acceptable **only as a body face** at a specific weight and tracking (see below). It is **not** the display face. The display face is a separate file, loaded with `font-display: block` (not swap), because a premium display face that flashes in as a fallback is worse than waiting for it.

**English display face:** paired, not matched. English display is set in a face that *answers* the Hebrew display face — same rhythm, different culture. Pangram Pangram, Klim Type Foundry, ABC Dinamo, Commercial Type, or Grilli Type. Not a Google Fonts serif. Not Instrument Serif. Not DM Serif.

**Body faces:**
- Hebrew body: Frank Ruhl Libre at 500 weight (not 400), with +0.005em tracking, line-height 1.75 at comfortable reading size. The default 400 weight reads as timid; 500 reads as ink.
- English body: a refined but non-reflex serif — candidates include Klim's *Tiempos Text*, Commercial's *Canela Text*, or Pangram's *Editorial New*. Not Lora, not Crimson, not Source Serif.

**Scale:** 1.25 modular ratio, five steps, fluid via `clamp()` on display and fixed `rem` on body. The scale is named by role (`--type-display`, `--type-h1`, `--type-h2`, `--type-body`, `--type-caption`), never by size.

**Numerals:** old-style figures (OpenType `onum`) in body and prose. Lining figures (`lnum`) only in tabular UI contexts. Fractions, ligatures, and contextual alternates enabled by default — we are paying for the font, we use what it has.

**Chapter headings are never centered.** They are set asymmetrically against the reading column — typically flushed to the opposite edge of the column with a generous optical overhang of 1–2 characters into the margin. The chapter number is typeset separately from the chapter title, often at a different scale, often on a different baseline. Centering a chapter title is a default; this product refuses defaults.

#### 4. Sanctuary, not Browser (chrome)

The product has no standard navbar. The header that currently exists in `src/components/Header.astro` — sticky, backdrop-blur, logo on one side, toggles on the other — is a generic web-app pattern and is rejected by this brief. A sanctuary has a threshold, not a toolbar.

**Rules:**

- **No persistent top bar on a reading page.** The chapter's title is part of the page, set in the display face, anchored to the top of the reading column. Navigation is invoked by the reader (keystroke, edge gesture, cursor-to-edge reveal), not presented as standing furniture.
- **No standard scrollbar, anywhere.** The native Webkit / Firefox scrollbar is replaced by a bespoke reading-rail that is invisible by default and appears only during active scroll, fading out on dwell. On the Density Map it becomes a moving ink-mark. It is never a thin gray strip.
- **No modals for trivia.** Any confirmation, toast, or onboarding tip that currently uses `position: fixed; z-index: 9999; backdrop-filter: blur(...)` is subject to removal. The only acceptable full-screen interrupt is "you are about to lose data"; everything else finds another surface.
- **No floating action buttons as primary navigation.** The current FAB stack (`#tts-fab`, `#bm-fab-btn`, `#stats-fab-btn`, `#highlights-fab`) clutters the lower-inline edge of every reading page and reads as dashboard chrome. Each of those actions must be reconsidered: does it belong in a reader-invoked panel (slides in from the outer edge, dismisses on click-away) rather than a permanent circle? TTS may retain a single surfaced control during active playback — never idle.
- **No generic drop shadows.** The pattern `box-shadow: 0 Npx Npx -Npx rgba(0,0,0,0.N)` is an AI tell. Elevation is expressed through paper — a slight warm underlay, a hairline border that tracks the atmosphere, or a subtle grain offset — not a soft black blur.
- **No side-stripe borders on list items / cards / callouts.** `border-inline-start: 3px solid …` is explicitly banned by the skill and must be removed from `p.bm-marked` in [src/styles/theme.css](src/styles/theme.css).
- **No gradient decorations on bookmarks, highlights, or backgrounds.** The current `linear-gradient(to right, #fbbf24 …, transparent)` treatment on bookmarked paragraphs is replaced by the ink-on-paper bleed filter.

### Design Principles

Five principles. Every design decision on this project is checked against these, in order. If principle 1 conflicts with principle 5, principle 1 wins.

1. **The page is the product.** The text is the only thing on screen that is allowed to be loud. Everything else is quiet until invoked.
2. **Atmosphere, not modes.** State that affects the reader's visual field is continuous, ambient, and context-aware. Discrete toggles are a symptom of lazy thinking.
3. **Carved typography, never rendered.** Type is selected, tracked, weighted, and spaced as an object. Reflex fonts (Inter, Roboto, Fraunces, Newsreader, Lora, Crimson, Playfair, Cormorant, IBM Plex, Space Grotesk, DM *, Outfit, Plus Jakarta, Instrument *, Syne — per the skill's banned list) are disqualified on sight.
4. **One motion language, four curves.** Every `transition` and `animation` in the codebase references one of four named curves — `--ease-standard`, `--ease-emphasized`, `--ease-accelerated`, `--ease-spring`. No raw `ease`, no raw `linear`, no ad-hoc `cubic-bezier(...)` inline. No bounce, no elastic.
5. **Docs-as-System™.** Every visible decision on screen traces to a `--yuval-*` token. If it's in CSS but not in the token system, it's a bug. If it's in the token system but not used, it's dead wood. The design system is the code, not a Figma file.

## Execution Notes (binding, not decorative)

These are the implementation-level commitments that fall out of the principles above. They are included so that future sessions have a concrete "to-do" shape and not just a manifesto.

- **Color tokens move to OKLCH.** The current `#rrggbb` triplets in [src/styles/theme.css](src/styles/theme.css) are transitional. OKLCH lets the atmosphere interpolate perceptually uniformly; hex does not. Neutrals are tinted toward the First Light paper hue (roughly `oklch(… … 75deg)`), not kept at pure gray.
- **Motion tokens — the four curves:**
  - `--ease-standard: cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1)` — default for state changes (already the project's informal default).
  - `--ease-emphasized: cubic-bezier(0.3, 0, 0, 1)` — larger, more deliberate movements (panels, reveals).
  - `--ease-accelerated: cubic-bezier(0.3, 0, 1, 1)` — exits, dismissals.
  - `--ease-spring: linear(…)` — the atmosphere drift; expressed as a CSS `linear()` easing sampled from a critically-damped spring, not a bouncy one.
- **Spacing tokens:** 4pt scale, semantic names (`--space-xs` through `--space-3xl`), via `clamp()` where page-level. No `p-8` magic numbers in markup when the spacing is carrying hierarchy.
- **Existing code audit** (non-exhaustive, for reference, to be done as a separate `/polish` pass):
  - Remove side-stripe border on `p.bm-marked` in [src/styles/theme.css](src/styles/theme.css).
  - Remove `#fbbf24`, `#ef4444`, `#2563eb` literal colors from theme.css — they are foreign to the atmosphere.
  - Replace `ease` defaults on `a, button` with `--ease-standard`.
  - Replace generic `box-shadow` drops on FAB stack, `#hl-panel`, `#bm-panel`, `#ob-tooltip` with paper-underlay treatment.
  - Reconsider the FAB stack entirely (see principle 1).
  - Replace backdrop-blur overlays with atmosphere-native dimming (interpolated surface token, not a rgba wash).
- **i18n discipline.** Every user-facing string goes through `src/i18n/translations.ts`. This is pre-existing project policy and is not relaxed by the design brief.
- **Accessibility floor.** WCAG AA contrast at *every point along the atmosphere curve*, not just at the endpoints. Interpolation is validated at five sampled points (0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0). `prefers-reduced-motion` disables drift but not the atmosphere itself. Focus rings are visible against all atmosphere values.

## What this document is for

When any `/impeccable`, `/polish`, `/typeset`, `/layout`, or related skill runs, it reads this section first and uses it as the binding brief. Suggestions that violate any principle above are rejected, not negotiated. Suggestions that weaken any directive ("what if we also added a standard navbar as an option?") are rejected, not negotiated.

The product's advantage is its refusal. Every refusal in this document is load-bearing.
