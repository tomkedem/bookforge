# Machine Learning

A subset of artificial intelligence

• A type of AI that enables systems to **learn and improve from experience** without explicit programming of every step.

• The focus is on developing software that can access **data** and use it to **learn on their own.**

• What distinguishes them from regular software is that I teach my software to learn from examples – for example, I show it many pictures of cats or dogs. I don't tell the machine this is a cat and this is a dog - it learns on its own when I show it pictures of thousands of cats or dogs.

**Concept Map (according to the illustration):**

AI is the outermost envelope, and within it are sub-groups. Here's an explanation of each of the seven components:


| Layer | What Is It? | Why Is It There? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AI | The big umbrella | The attempt to make a machine mimic intelligence (even without learning). |
| ML | Learning from data | The computer's ability to infer rules from data. |
| Neural Networks | The mathematical structure | Algorithms that try to mimic the function of neurons in the brain. |
| Deep Learning | Deep learning | Using neural networks with many layers to process complex data. |
| Generative AI | Content creation | The stage where the machine doesn't just classify, but generates (text, code). |
| LLMs | Language models | The most powerful implementation of GenAI today (like GPT). |
| NLP | The arrow crossing out | Natural Language Processing: this is the human need. The reason it crosses all of them is that we used to solve it with "AI rules," then with "ML statistics," and today we solve it with "LLMs." It's the task, everything else are the tools. |
