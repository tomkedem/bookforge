**Point to Consider Before the Course:**

**Class Preparation:** The course seems intensive ("Flipped Classroom"). The real value will come from in-class discussions on edge cases, not frontal lectures.

During the course, we'll use Cursor extensively. Whoever wants can use something else. Additionally, we'll use APIs, you'll create keys for some of them in Anthropic models, also in Gemini, it's worth investing a few dollars to get access to models. We'll also use Hipo 1.5, which is very cheap.

It's good for everyone to have accounts because it's just pennies and it will pay off in salary. Sometimes we'll need to change keys or download models, you also need an AWS account - we'll set up various things there, maybe spin up a small server.

You need to know how to do these things. We'll combine theory with practice - in the first part we'll talk about theory and in the second part we'll dive into real examples. It's important to understand everything.

And we'll have a final project - some kind of agent that provides automatic service.

I suggest organizing into groups to do assignments in groups. Even homework is recommended to do in groups. When we're in class, it will be easier to connect.

**Course Objectives**

• **Planning and implementing production-ready AI applications:**

Moving from the experiment and demo stage to stable and reliable systems in the real world.

• **Effectively integrating LLMs (language models):**

Learning the right ways to embed models within existing architecture.

• **Building advanced AI Agents:**

Developing autonomous systems capable of planning and executing complex tasks.

• **Implementing MLOps best practices:**

Implementing Continuous Delivery processes and model lifecycle management.

• **Advanced Context Engineering:**

Controlling the information presented to the model to maximize accuracy and minimize "noise."

• **Security, costs, and optimization:**

Managing information security risks, controlling token budgets, and improving system performance.

• **Distinguishing between Fundamentals and Buzzwords/frameworks:**

Developing the ability to identify core technologies versus passing trends. Things change all the time and therefore it's important to know the principles and how to learn them without fear – you always need to keep learning and constantly upgrade yourself because nowadays things are updated on a weekly basis.

• **Building the right Mindset for success in the AI era:**

Adopting a flexible way of thinking adapted to rapid changes in the field.

**Note:** that the objectives here don't just talk about "how to write code," but about the complete envelope: **security, costs, and MLOps**. As people working in organizations, these are the points where projects usually fail. We should emphasize optimization and security topics from the very first stages of our final project.
