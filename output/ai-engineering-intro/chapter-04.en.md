# Common Use Cases


| Scenario | Business/Technical Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Repeat Calls | Identifying customers who call again and again on the same topic. The customer is probably not satisfied and therefore calls repeatedly. If 20% of calls are repeat calls, they can save a lot of money. Goal: to improve FCR (First Call Resolution) and thereby save money. |
| Long Calls | Analysis of unusually long calls to understand where the agent was delayed or where the process is too cumbersome. |
| Self-Service | Identifying topics that can be transferred to bots or to the personal area on the website to reduce the load on the human call center and save costs. |
| Customer Churn | Analyzing early warning signs in the conversation (such as threats to leave or extreme dissatisfaction) to prevent the customer from leaving. It's like a balloon - on one side I increase the air, there are customers joining, and on the other side I decrease the air, there are customers leaving, and I want to increase the air in the balloon. |
| Sentiment (or Mood) | Measuring the customer's happiness or anger level throughout the call. |
| Sales | Identifying opportunities for additional sales (Upsell) or analyzing closing techniques of top-performing agents. |
| Improvement (Performance/Processes) | A general term for system optimization, agent training, and call center efficiency. |



**Professional Analysis:**

1. **Repeat Calls and Long Calls:** Very easy to create dashboards on them that show immediate value (ROI) to management.

2. **Self-Service Optimization:** This is where NLP and data clustering come in. The system identifies which questions repeat themselves enough that it's worth developing automation for them.

3. **Churn Prediction:** This is one of the most complex ML scenarios. It requires Data Enrichment between the call text and the customer's CRM data (how long they've been with the company, how much they pay, etc.).
