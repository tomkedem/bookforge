# AI Engineer Skills Part 2




| Skill | What Does This Mean in Practice? | Added Value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Evaluation and Observability | Implementing rigorous testing frameworks and monitoring tools (Tracing) to measure model accuracy. | Measurement not guessing: the ability to know in real-time if the model in Production is safe and accurate. |
| ML Fundamentals | Conceptual understanding of Embeddings and model architectures. The more we understand, the better we can intuit what will work and what won't. | Informed decision-making: knowing when to use Prompting and when Fine-tuning is really needed. |
| MLOps - Infrastructure & Deployment | Managing cloud environments, containers (Docker), and cost optimization strategies. | Scalability: the ability to run AI workloads efficiently and cost-effectively in the cloud. |
| Product and Problem Thinking | You need to know the problem and the product you're working on and translate the product into design – product managers don't always understand the limitations and boundaries. You need to know how to translate this into realistic things. Ultimately, it's about translating vague user requirements into technical AI designs. | Business connection: knowing how to identify when AI is indeed the right solution for a problem (and when it's not). |
| Safety and Responsible AI | Reducing Hallucinations and biases through Guardrails. You need to know how to deal with regulation even though it might seem like a headache. We need to do everything to prevent intrusions into all our development. | Ethics and security: meeting strict standards for data privacy and filtering unwanted responses. |
